Why you need Technical Document?

A designer might have to work on a project for an extended period of time. When the time for delivery comes, they might have to look back at their process and document it. Which makes it a bit difficult as you have to recall the things which you have done a few months before. To avoid such a scenario let’s understand why UX documentation is so important.

  1. To keep multiple designers working on the same project collaborate — teammates can see each other’s updates if they are continuously adding their task data and analysis in a shared document. Using platforms like FIGJAM, Microsoft Word or Google docs is the best example of such practices.
  2. UX documentation with few edits can be used as a case study — you might have often come across companies that present their past projects as a form of a case study which is nothing but a small portion of their design documentation that showcases how they solved the challenge with their UX process.
  3. It can be considered as an additional deliverable —not all clients request full UX process as deliverables. But when working for a freelance client where you are also building up a trust relationship for future projects, I have observed that UX documentation always acts as a cherry on top of other deliverables. It also adds a professional touch to your task.
  4. UX documentation with few edits can be used as a case study — you might have often come across companies that present their past projects as a form of a case study which is nothing but a small portion of their design documentation that showcases how they solved the challenge with their UX process.
  5. It helps new onboarding designers — people who join the project midway need lots of KT and exploration not just about the product but also about our approach as designers to solve them. I have observed that when a team of designers is documenting the UX process side by side, then the documented data can be referred to catch up to the ongoing process in a much faster way
  6. It also gives a roadmap for future updates — in the future if the project is evolving the revampers often look for legacy research approaches and UI data.


Importance of technical documentation

Technical documentation is vitally important for your software company. Here are some reasons
Enables quick decision-making by the product team
When your product team has access to the right technical documentation, they can make much quicker decisions. They don’t have to scroll back through emails or threads in collaboration tools – they can instead instantly consult the documents produced alongside the software that explains how everything works and records the reasoning behind the decisions.

Help for users
When customers are using their software they can access your technical documentation alongside the product for help in using the tool. Documentation can be displayed in-app so customers don’t have to switch contexts when they run into issues. This improves the overall usability and experience of your software product.
